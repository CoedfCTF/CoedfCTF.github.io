About the Organiser

Center of Excellence in Digital Forensics

The Center of Excellence in Digital Forensics (COEDF), based in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, is a premier institution dedicated to advancing cybersecurity education, digital forensics training, and hands-on security research for the next generation of cyber professionals.

Founded with the mission to bridge the gap between academic curriculum and real-world security challenges, COEDF offers cutting-edge programs in cyber forensics, penetration testing, threat intelligence, and incident response — equipping students and professionals with the practical skills that today's industry demands.

Our state-of-the-art lab facility at Perungudi, Chennai provides an immersive environment where learners work with real tools, real attack scenarios, and real infrastructure — mirroring what they will encounter in the field.

Event Information

Event Overview

Everything you need to know about COEDF CTF 2026

🎯
What is CTF?
Capture The Flag (CTF) is a cybersecurity competition where participants solve hacking challenges to find hidden flags — secret strings embedded inside vulnerable apps, files, or systems.
🏫
Hosted by COEDF
Organised by the Center of Excellence in Digital Forensics, Chennai — a premier institution dedicated to advancing cybersecurity education and hands-on technical training.
📍
Offline Format
Fully in-person competition. All teams must be physically present. You'll compete on a dedicated isolated lab network with real challenge infrastructure.
👥
Team Participation
Compete in teams of exactly 3 members. Mixed-skill teams are encouraged — pairing security experts with beginners leads to the best outcomes.
🔐
Flag Format
All flags follow the format coedf{...}. Flags are case-sensitive. Submit exactly as found. Each challenge has a unique, one-time-use flag.
📶
Infrastructure
Challenges hosted on a local CTF platform with a live scoreboard. Participants bring their own laptops with preferred security tools pre-installed.

Participation Paths

Participation Tracks

Two dedicated tracks so both newcomers and veterans have a rewarding experience

🌱  Beginner Friendly
Starter Track
For first-time CTF participants and students from non-security backgrounds. Focus on core concepts with helpful hints, guided problems, and mentor support throughout the event.
  • Introduction to web, crypto, and steganography
  • Guided problem statements with structured hints
  • Tool recommendations and setup support
  • Mentor assistance during the event
  • Certificate of participation for all starters
  • Ideal for B.Tech / MCA first and second years
⚡  Advanced Track
Elite Track
Built for experienced security professionals, researchers, and competitive CTF players. No hints. No hand-holding. Raw challenges demanding expertise across multiple domains.
  • Binary exploitation and reverse engineering
  • Custom cryptographic implementations to break
  • Multi-stage attack chains across services
  • Zero-hint hard and insane challenges
  • Mobile and network security challenges
  • Full points — eligible for all prize categories

Compliance

Rules & Guidelines

All participants must read and comply. Violations result in immediate disqualification.

01
No Attacking the Infrastructure. Do not attack the CTF platform, scoring server, other teams' machines, or any system outside the designated challenge scope.
02
No Flag Sharing. Flags must not be shared between teams. Each team must solve challenges independently using their own skills.
03
No Brute Forcing. Automated brute-force attacks against login portals, flags, or APIs are strictly prohibited.
04
Team Size is Fixed. Teams must consist of exactly 3 members. Last-minute member changes are not permitted after registration is confirmed.
05
Respect Fellow Participants. Harassment, discrimination, or unsportsmanlike behaviour of any kind will result in immediate removal.
06
Flag Format is Mandatory. All flags must be submitted in the exact format coedf{...}. Flags are case-sensitive.
07
Organiser Decisions are Final. In disputes, ties, or system issues, the organisers' decision is binding and final.
08
Bring Your Own Device. Participants must bring their own laptops with required tools. Kali Linux is recommended. No shared devices.
09
Hint Deductions. Using a hint deducts points from the challenge reward. Once used, hints cannot be undone.
10
No External Collaboration. Getting help from anyone outside your registered team — online or offline — during the event is strictly prohibited.
